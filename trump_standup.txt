TRUMP STANDUP SPECIAL — "TREMENDOUS PROBLEMS"
A 10-Minute Stand-Up Routine

SETTING: A comedy club stage. Single spotlight. Brick wall backdrop. A microphone on a stand. A stool with a glass of water. American flag pin on lapel.

=========================================

FADE IN

INT. COMEDY CLUB — NIGHT

(Trump walks out to the microphone in his navy blue suit and red tie, waving with both hands, pointing at people in the audience)

TRUMP
Thank you, thank you. What a crowd. What a beautiful crowd. You know, people say to me, "Sir, why are you doing stand-up?" And I say, because nobody talks about the real problems. The everyday problems. The problems that affect you, that affect me, that affect everybody. And frankly, nobody talks about them better than me. Nobody.

(adjusts microphone, leans in)

TRUMP
Let's talk about the toaster. Okay? The toaster. I have a toaster — beautiful toaster, top of the line, very expensive — and every single morning, every morning, it burns one side of the bread and the other side is basically raw dough. Every. Single. Morning. I'm standing there in my robe, a very beautiful robe by the way, silk, and I'm looking at this toast thinking, "How? How is this possible?" We put a man on the moon. We did that. But we can't make toast? Both sides? Evenly? It's a disgrace.

(takes a step back, gestures broadly)

TRUMP
And you can't return it. You know why? Because the receipt — the receipt is gone. It's always gone. I have people, smart people, the best people, and nobody can find the receipt. It's like it evaporates. The Deep State of receipts.

(pauses for effect, takes a sip of water)

TRUMP
Now let me tell you about WiFi. I know more about WiFi than probably anybody. And my WiFi at home — which is, by the way, a very large home, very beautiful — the WiFi stops working the moment you need it. The exact moment. You're trying to watch something, something very important, and suddenly it's buffering. That little circle going around and around. I call it the Circle of Sadness. Very sad. Many people are saying that.

(walks to the other side of the stage)

TRUMP
And then they tell you, "Restart the router." Restart the router! Like I'm some kind of IT guy. I'm not an IT guy. I'm a businessman. A very successful businessman. But now I'm on my hands and knees behind a cabinet, unplugging things, plugging them back in, and my wife is yelling from the other room, "Is it working yet?" No! No, it's not working yet! It takes two minutes! Why does it take two minutes? What is it doing for two minutes? Nobody knows. It's a mystery. Bigger mystery than the Bermuda Triangle, frankly.

(straightens tie, looks directly at audience)

TRUMP
Let's talk about printers. Printers are — and I say this with great authority — printers are the worst invention in the history of mankind. Worse than — no, I won't say it. But they're bad. Very bad. You press print, nothing happens. You press it again, nothing. You press it fourteen times. Nothing. Then you give up, you walk away, and an hour later it prints fourteen copies. Fourteen! Of your bank statement. And your kid finds them. Great. Fantastic. Very cool.

(mimics pressing a button repeatedly)

TRUMP
And the ink. Don't get me started on the ink. Printer ink costs more per gallon than human blood. I looked it up. Well, I had someone look it up. And they came back and said, "Sir, it's true." Of course it's true. I'm always right about these things. A gallon of ink costs more than a gallon of champagne. Good champagne. French champagne. And the cartridge, the little cartridge, says it's empty when it's not empty. There's ink in there! I can see it! I can shake it and hear it! But the printer says no. The printer has decided. The printer is the boss now. Not me. The printer.

(sits on the stool, crosses legs)

TRUMP
You know what else? Grocery stores. The self-checkout. Whose idea was that? I'll tell you whose idea — someone who hates people. Because now I'm standing there, I'm scanning my own groceries like an employee, except I'm not getting paid. I'm paying them. And the machine is yelling at me. "Unexpected item in the bagging area." Unexpected? It's a banana! There's nothing unexpected about a banana! It's the most expected item in the entire store!

(stands up, animated)

TRUMP
And then it locks up. The light starts flashing. Now everyone's looking at me. Everyone. And some teenager has to come over with a key — a key! — and they look at you like you're stupid. Like you don't know how to buy bananas. I've bought bananas in forty countries. I know how to buy bananas. The machine is broken. The machine is the problem. Not me. Never me.

(walks back to center stage)

TRUMP
Let me tell you about phone calls. I get a call, I look at my phone, it says "Scam Likely." Scam Likely! That's the name. The phone has already decided this person is a scam and it's still letting the call through. Why? Block it! You know it's a scam, I know it's a scam, we all know it's a scam. But no, the phone wants me to make the decision. I have enough decisions to make. I don't need this one.

(holds up imaginary phone)

TRUMP
And voicemail. I have four hundred voicemails. Four hundred. I'm never listening to them. Never. If it was important, they'd call back. Or text. Or send a carrier pigeon. I don't care. But the little red badge, the notification, the number that says four hundred — it haunts me. It's always there. Staring at me. Judging me. Four hundred unheard messages. And somehow that makes me the bad guy.

(loosens imaginary tie, gets animated)

TRUMP
Now, GPS. Let's talk about GPS. I love GPS, in theory. Great concept. But mine, and this is true, mine told me to turn into a lake. A lake! It said, "Turn right in two hundred feet." And two hundred feet later, it's water. Just water. A whole lake. And the GPS is still saying, "Continue on the current route." The current route is drowning! That's the route! The GPS is trying to assassinate me and nobody is investigating this.

(paces back and forth)

TRUMP
And it recalculates. You miss one turn, one turn, and it gets very passive-aggressive. "Recalculating." That tone. You hear the tone? It's disappointed in you. Your own GPS is disappointed in you. And then it adds twenty minutes to your trip out of spite. I'm convinced it adds extra time as punishment. "Oh, you didn't turn? Enjoy this detour through a neighborhood that doesn't exist on any map."

(comes back to the mic, grips the stand)

TRUMP
You know what nobody talks about? Fitted sheets. Putting on a fitted sheet is the hardest thing a human being can do. Harder than — look, I've done deals, big deals, the biggest deals in the world, and none of them were as difficult as putting on a fitted sheet. You get one corner on, you go to the next corner, the first corner pops off. Every time. It's like the sheet is fighting you. It's a war. And the sheet is winning. The sheet is undefeated.

(mimics wrestling with a sheet)

TRUMP
And don't tell me to fold one. Don't. People say, "Oh, you just tuck the corners into each other." No. No, you don't. That's a lie. Nobody can fold a fitted sheet. Martha Stewart can't fold a fitted sheet. She pretends she can but she can't. I asked her. She said, "Donald, I just roll it into a ball." A ball! Martha Stewart! The queen of home stuff! She rolls it into a ball like the rest of us. We're all frauds when it comes to fitted sheets. All of us.

(takes a final sip of water)

TRUMP
And one more thing — one more very important thing — alarm clocks. Setting the alarm clock on your phone. There's AM and PM. Why is it so easy to mess up AM and PM? I set my alarm for seven, I think it's seven in the morning, but it's actually seven at night. So now I'm sleeping through everything. I had a very important meeting, very big meeting, and I missed it because Apple can't make a clock that makes sense. And then people say, "Use military time." Military time! I'm not in the military! I shouldn't need a math degree to wake up at the right time!

(walks to front of stage, arms open)

TRUMP
But you know what? Despite all of it — the toasters, the WiFi, the printers, the fitted sheets — we keep going. We get up every day. We fight the toaster. We restart the router. We scan our own bananas. Because that's what we do. We're fighters. Every one of you. Tremendous fighters. The best fighters.

(points to audience)

TRUMP
Thank you. You've been a beautiful audience. The best audience. Better than any audience anywhere, ever. Nobody has ever had an audience this good. Goodnight!

(waves, walks offstage to spotlight fading)

FADE OUT
